We need to find corner label configurations where it's *guaranteed* that the two distinct classes meet *inside* the square. This means we're looking for situations where the classes are "intertwined" in a way that they *must* cross each other, not just touch at the edges.

**Canonicalization**

The canonicalization process means that the absolute values of the labels don't matter, only their relative distinctness. For example, (2, 0, 3, 1) would be treated the same as (0, 1, 2, 3) or (1, 0, 3, 2). We relabel based on the order of appearance: first encountered label becomes 0, the next *new* label becomes 1.

**Scenarios where classes *must* meet inside**

Consider the colors of the corners. If the colors of diagonally opposite corners are different, it forces the two classes to meet within the square. Think of it this way: if you start at one corner with class A and travel towards its diagonal opposite corner of class B, you *must* cross the boundary between A and B somewhere in the interior.

The pairs of diagonally opposite corners are:

So, if the labels of BL and TR are different, OR the labels of BR and TL are different, then the two classes *must* meet inside.
