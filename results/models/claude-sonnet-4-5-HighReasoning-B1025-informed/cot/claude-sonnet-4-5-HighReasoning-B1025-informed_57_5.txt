I need to find two straight segments whose endpoints lie on the boundary of the unit square, where the coordinates are from {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1}. These two segments, together with the square's edges, must partition the interior into exactly 4 quadrilaterals.

Let me think about this geometrically. If I have two segments, they could:
1. Intersect inside the square - this would create 4 regions
2. Not intersect - this would create 3 regions

For exactly 4 quadrilaterals, the two segments must intersect inside the square.

Let me try a simple example:
- Segment 1: from (0, 0.5) to (1, 0.5) - horizontal line through the middle
- Segment 2: from (0.5, 0) to (0.5, 1) - vertical line through the middle

These two segments intersect at (0.5, 0.5) and divide the square into 4 quadrilaterals (rectangles):
- Bottom-left: corners (0,0), (0.5,0), (0.5,0.5), (0,0.5)
- Bottom-right: corners (0.5,0), (1,0), (1,0.5), (0.5,0.5)
- Top-left: corners (0,0.5), (0.5,0.5), (0.5,1), (0,1)
- Top-right: corners (0.5,0.5), (1,0.5), (1,1), (0.5,1)

All four regions are quadrilaterals. This works!
