Square 1: (2, 1, 2, 1)
- Top-left: 2, Bottom-left: 1, Bottom-right: 2, Top-right: 1
- Opposite corners: TL(2) vs BR(2) are same class, BL(1) vs TR(1) are same class
- This creates a saddle configuration with two classes
- The connection is deterministic: class 2 connects top-left to bottom-right, class 1 connects bottom-left to top-right
- This is "known behaviour"

Square 2: (4, 1, 2, 3)

- We have three distinct classes meeting in this square
- This is "three domains meeting"
