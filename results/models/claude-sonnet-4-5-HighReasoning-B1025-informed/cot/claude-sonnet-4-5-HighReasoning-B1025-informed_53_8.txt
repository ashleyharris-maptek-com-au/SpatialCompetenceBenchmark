For each square (TL, BR, BL, TR):

The key distinction emerges when I look at the corner patterns more carefully. Cases with deterministic topologies occur when corners have distinct arrangements, while the ambiguous case appears specifically with saddle configurations—diagonal patterns where only two classes alternate. In configurations like (3, 1, 1, 2), three classes with one repeated creates a deterministic topology. The saddle pattern (2, 2, 1, 1) with two classes in opposite corners produces multiple valid topologies. Cases with three or four distinct classes at corners consistently yield deterministic solutions.
