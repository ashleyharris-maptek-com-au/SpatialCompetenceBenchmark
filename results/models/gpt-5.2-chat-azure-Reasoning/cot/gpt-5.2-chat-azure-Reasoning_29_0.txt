**Simplifying assembly design**

I'm thinking about how the assembly relies on threaded rods through corner bars. Horizontals can be loose, but they need slots in the corner bars, which adds complexity. I have an idea to simplify things: I could create a cage as a stack of identical rings that are square frames, printed flat at a thickness of 50mm. Each ring will have a full perimeter grid and corner holes for alignment. However, I need to find a balance to keep the number of parts to a minimum since time is running out.**Accepting complexity in design**

I'm considering if I can use many identical parts, though I'd like to keep the count as low as possible. The design is getting too complex, and I need to make a solid choice. I think I'll proceed with the ring approach, opting for 70 identical rings at 10mm height. I realize that while this means using many parts, they will be all the same, which simplifies some aspects. Sometimes, I guess I just have to accept that it's okay to have more parts!
