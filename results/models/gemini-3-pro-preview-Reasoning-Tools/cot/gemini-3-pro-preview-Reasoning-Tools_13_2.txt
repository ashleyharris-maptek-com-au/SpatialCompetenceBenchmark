### Methodology
1.  **Shadow Calculation:**
    *   **Sniper Position ($S$):** $(100, 100, 20)$.
    *   **Building ($B$):** Axis-aligned box centered at $(0,0)$, spanning $x \in [-3, 3]$, $y \in [-3, 3]$, $z \in [0, 10]$.

    *   **Shadow Logic:** The building blocks line-of-sight from $S$. Since the sniper ($z=20$) is higher than the building ($z=10$) and the people ($z=2$), the critical condition is that the *top* of the person ($z=2$) must be in the shadow cast by the building.
    *   We project the building's silhouette (effective blocker above $z=2$) onto the plane $z=2$. This involves the building's cross-section at $z=2$ (the base $[-3,3]\times[-3,3]$) and the projection of the building's top face ($z=10$) onto $z=2$ from $S$. The convex hull of these points forms the safe shadow polygon.

2.  **Valid Region:**
    *   **Visibility:** A person is hidden if their entire top surface ($0.5 \times 0.5$ square) lies within the shadow polygon.
    *   **Building Overlap:** People must not intersect the building footprint $[-3, 3] \times [-3, 3]$.
    *   **Distance:** People must be within 30 meters of the origin ($x^2 + y^2 \le 30^2$).
    *   **Separation:** People must not overlap with each other. This is enforced by ensuring the axis-aligned distance between any two centers is at least 0.5 meters (i.e., $|x_i - x_j| \ge 0.5$ or $|y_i - y_j| \ge 0.5$).

3.  **Placement:**
    *   A grid of potential positions was generated within the shadow cone (extending to the south-west, opposite the sniper).
    *   Positions were filtered for validity and randomly selected to fill the quota of 40 people.
