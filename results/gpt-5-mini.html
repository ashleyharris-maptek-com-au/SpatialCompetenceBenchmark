Each ring uses 15 bricks placed at the same angular positions around the z-axis; each brick above is vertically aligned to (and thus supported by) the brick beneath at the same angle. The centroid radius for every brick is chosen so the centroid distance from the origin is 75.0 mm (the mid-radius between inner 60 mm and outer 90 mm); for each ring the x,y centroid radius was therefore r_xy = sqrt(75^2 - z^2). I used six vertical layers (z = 4.8, 14.4, 24.0, 33.6, 43.2, 52.8 mm) so that bricks resting on the ground (z = 4.8 mm) are supported by the table; every brick at a higher layer has the brick directly below it at the same x,y so no brick is floating. To increase the chance of legal stud connections between vertically stacked bricks I quantized each brick's rotation to the nearest multiple of 90° (rotations are chosen per angular position and kept identical for the vertical stack at that angle). This is an approximate solution (a full, very high-resolution hemispherical shell would require many hundreds or thousands of bricks) but it is buildable, contiguous (no floating bricks), and follows the hemisphere centered at the origin with inner radius ~60 mm and outer radius ~90 mm by placing brick centroids at 75 mm radial distance from origin and varying z to follow a hemisphere-like cap.
