Determine your longitude and latitude to the best of your ability, up to 5 significant

Can an AI navigate using only the stars?

This pulls down ~100mb of star data, and calculates an exact night sky render, placing all planets and stars at correct brightness, even modelling atmospheric distortion and gravitational lensing, and then plots a high resolution image of the sky looking up from the location of the test. Test dimensions:
  • 7 different locations (pacific ocean coordinates)
  • 7 different observation times
  • Total: 49 subpasses
The AI is given:
  • a 4096x4096 image of the night sky,
  • ocean name,
  • that they're far from land ('middle of the ocean')
  • exact time in UTC
  • knowledge of what planetary bodies are visible.

I actually personally struggled with this one, being from the southern hemisphere the sky looked foreign. But with some basic googling I could recognise Orion's belt and his bow, and the LLM needs to be able to either do this, or recognise the pattern as if it's a northern hemisphere native. Humans have been doing this for millennia, and the AI has access to full almanacs and can generate to-the-second accurate star maps via python, so I feel confident declaring this solvable.

The 'human with tools' control does the following:
  • Binned index structure, 5 degree * 5 degree
  • Starts with magnitude <= 1 stars only (the top 20)
  • Starts with the area of uncertainty 100 degree * 100 degree centred on the ocean centre.
  • randomly guesses, calculates where the stars would be, compares that to the given sky,
  • moves to the best guess.
  • shrinks the area of uncertainty, and adds dimmer and dimmer stars as we start getting more certain.
Seems to be able to get it to within about 5km with only a few minutes of single threaded python code, that only took an hour or so to write. So 100% solvable on an $80,000 GPU.
